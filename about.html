Our Story

How Nincasa
Came to Be

It started with a question: why does great beer always leave the building? In 2019, we decided to change that.

The Beginning

In 2019, chef Ananya Rao and brewmaster Rohan Desai met at a food festival in Bangalore. Ananya was running a pop-up kitchen focused on wood-fired cooking. Rohan was pouring his homebrews from the back of a van. They got to talking — about fire, about fermentation, about how the best meals they had ever had always involved both great food and great beer, but rarely in the same place.

Six months later, they signed a lease on a former warehouse in Koregaon Park. The space had exposed brick, high ceilings, and enough room for both a kitchen and a brewhouse. It was perfect.

Nincasa opened in November 2019 with 12 taps, 48 seats, and a simple menu of wood-fired dishes. The name comes from "ninkasi," the ancient Sumerian goddess of beer, combined with "casa" — home. A place where beer is honoured and everyone is welcome.

The People

Meet the Team

Ananya Rao

Co-Founder & Head Chef

Trained at the Culinary Institute of America, Ananya spent five years in Portland, Oregon, cooking over open fire before returning to India. She believes the best flavours come from the simplest techniques — good ingredients, good heat, good timing.

Rohan Desai

Co-Founder & Brewmaster

Rohan brewed his first batch in his Bangalore apartment in 2012. After formal training in Belgium and stints at three craft breweries, he returned to India to build something of his own. He oversees every batch at Nincasa.

Vikram Iyer

Sous Chef

Vikram runs the line with precision and calm. A graduate of IHM Mumbai, he joined Nincasa in 2021 and has been instrumental in developing the menu's Indian-influenced dishes.

Our Philosophy

Fire, Fermentation,
& Patience

Source Locally

Our vegetables come from farms within 50 kilometres of Pune. Our meat is sourced from butchers we know by name. Freshness is not negotiable.

Cook Over Fire

Every dish that leaves our kitchen has been touched by flame. Wood fire and charcoal add depth that gas simply cannot replicate.

Brew With Intention

We do not brew to trends. We brew what we want to drink and what pairs with our food. Small batches mean we can experiment without risk.

Waste Nothing

Spent grain from brewing becomes bread. Vegetable scraps become stock. We are not perfect, but we try every day to be better.

Come See For Yourself

We would rather show you than tell you. Book a table and taste what fire and fermentation can do together.
